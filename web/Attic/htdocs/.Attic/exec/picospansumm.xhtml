Summary of PicoSpan Commands

This page is under construction. (current update 18 April 94)

Valerie (popcorn) Mates posted the following information in item 1 of the info conference. I have harvested it and placed it here. I would welcome any attempts to add useful additional information to this page. -srw

  • To see a list of the items in a conference, type "browse".
  • To read item 17, type "read 17".
  • To read, say, items 17 and 20 through 23 in a conference, type "read 17, 20-23".
  • To read the items you haven't yet seen, type "read unseen". This is useful, say, if you join a conference with 10 items and you want to see all the items, not just items 1 and 10.
  • To add a response to an item, type "r" at the "Respond or pass?" prompt. Picospan displays a ">" prompt for you to enter text. Type your response. It's a good idea to press Enter (or Return) after every 75 characters or so. Picospan doesn't do word-wrap. When you are done entering text, type a period on a blank line, and press Enter (or Return).
  • To get out from the middle of an item you are reading, type your interrupt character. That's usually either the delete key or control-c, but you could have set it to any of a number of other keys when you ran the newuser program.
When your interrupt gets you to the "Respond or pass?" prompt, here are some of your options:
  • Hit Enter to go on to the next item, leaving this item marked read.
  • Type "forget" to never see this item again. (Later, you can type, for example, "unforget 12" at an "Ok:" prompt to bring item 12 back again)
  • Type "postpone" to leave the responses in the item the way they were before you looked at it today, and go on to the next item you are reading.
  • Type "new" to "postpone" the current item and get back to the "Ok:" prompt.
Now you're ready to go back to the previous menu and try out a picospan session. Have fun!
